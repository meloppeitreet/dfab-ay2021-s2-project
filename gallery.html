Computer-Aided Design (CAD)

Some exercises provided had allowed us to practice the use of hole, combine, and bevel tools in Fusion 360 to create objects of various shapes. This allowed us to gain a proper understanding in sketching and extruding mainly as well, preparing us for the 3D printed components of our final project.

Finally, we had created a pawn chess piece to use the rotate tool as well, acting as a precursor to the knight chess piece we were going to create for 3D printing.

During this time, we also got to touch on the use of bitmap tracing in 2D graphics.

3D Printing

We first created the 3D model of the knight chess piece in Fusion 360 using what we were taught in CAD, and then proceeded to slice the exported .3mf file to GCODE for the Ultimaker 3D printer using the CURA software.

As the 3D print had included support structures for overhangs in the design, it was necessary to cut them away, and I had also tried to file away the remaining of the supports.

Laser Cutting

After designing the box along with its edge teeth, we exported the design as a DXF file to inkscape for it to be combined and exported as a single SVG file for the laser cutter.

Red (255,0,0) had been for cuts, while green (0,255,0) had been for engraving of our design.

The hole for the music box handle, as well as the excess space between the back end of the lid and the back face of the box had to also be accounted for so that the box could be used for the music box and the so that the lid could rotate without hitting into the backface, giving us our final result.
